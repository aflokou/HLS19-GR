What does this chart show?

The HLS19-NAV-GR instrument has 12 items, each rated 1–4. There are 412 = 16,777,216 possible response patterns. Each pattern yields two scores:

P-type (polytomous): mean of the 12 ratings, rescaled to 0–100. Takes 37 distinct values.

D-type (dichotomous): percentage of items rated 3–4 ("easy"), yielding 13 fixed values (0, 8.3, 16.7 … 100).

Each coloured bubble represents one (P-type, D-type) combination. Bubble area is proportional to the percentage of all 16.7M patterns that fall in that cell. Colour encodes the direction of change after dichotomisation: D > P — higher D = P — no change D < P — lower

The dashed circle at position (P, P) on the diagonal shows the marginal percentage of patterns that produce that P-type value — i.e., how common this P-type score is across all possible response combinations.

By design, the Higher and Lower groups are perfectly symmetric (each ~41.9% of patterns), while ~16.1% show no change. The diagonal line D = P separates higher (above) from lower (below) transitions.
