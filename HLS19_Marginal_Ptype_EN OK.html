HLS19 Greek Versions — P-type (polytomous) Score: Marginal Distribution

How many response patterns yield each P-type score? · NAV (412) · COM (411) · VAC (44) · Flokou et al., Healthcare 2025

What does this chart show?

For each possible P-type score, the bar height shows how many of all theoretical response patterns produce that score. This is the marginal (unconditional) distribution of P-type scores across all 4k equally likely patterns.

P-type scores are computed as the mean of the k item ratings (1–4), rescaled to 0–100. Because items take 4 integer values, P-type can take many distinct values — more items means more possible means.

The distribution is approximately binomial, symmetric around 50, and unimodal for even k (NAV 12-item, VAC 4-item) but produces a flat double-peak for odd k (COM 11-item), because the exact midpoint P=50.0 is unreachable.
