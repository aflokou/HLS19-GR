What does this chart show?

The HLS19-COM-GR instrument measures communicative health literacy with physicians. It has 11 items, each rated 1–4. There are 411 = 4,194,304 possible response patterns. Each pattern yields two scores:

P-type (polytomous): mean of the 11 ratings, rescaled to 0–100. Takes 34 distinct values.

D-type (dichotomous): percentage of items rated 3–4 (“easy”), yielding 12 fixed values (0, 9.1, 18.2 … 100).

Each coloured bubble represents one (P-type, D-type) combination. Bubble area is proportional to the percentage of all 4,194,304 patterns in that cell. Colour encodes the direction of change after dichotomisation: D > P — higher D = P — no change D < P — lower

The dashed circle at (P, P) on the diagonal shows the marginal percentage of patterns that yield that P-type value. Across all patterns: Higher 41.59% · No change 16.82% · Lower 41.59%.
