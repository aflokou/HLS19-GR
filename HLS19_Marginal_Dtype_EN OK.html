HLS19 Greek Versions — D-type (dichotomous) Score: Marginal Distribution

How many binary response patterns yield each D-type score? · NAV (212) · COM (211) · VAC (24) · Flokou et al., Healthcare 2025

What does this chart show?

For each possible D-type score, the bar height shows how many of all theoretical binary response patterns produce that score. This is the marginal (unconditional) distribution of D-type scores across all 2k equally likely binary patterns.

D-type scores are computed by recoding each item as 0 (rated 1 or 2 — “difficult”) or 1 (rated 3 or 4 — “easy”), then taking the percentage of “easy” items × 100. With k items there are 2k possible binary patterns and exactly k+1 distinct D-type values. The distribution follows a Binomial(k, 0.5) distribution — symmetric around 50.

For odd k (COM 11-item), D=50.0 is unreachable (would require exactly 5.5 “easy” items), so the peak splits symmetrically into D=45.5 and D=54.5 — a mathematical artefact, not real bimodality.
